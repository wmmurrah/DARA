Basic Notation

\[ Y_{ij} = \beta_{0j} + e_{ij}, \tag{1} \]

\[ \beta_{0j} = \gamma_{00} + u_{0j}, \tag{2} \]

\[ Y_{ij} = \gamma_{00} + u_{0j} + e_{ij}. \tag{3} \]

Example of Simpsons Paradox

This example of Simpsons paradox is taken from Joseph Stevens, which is based on data from (Snijders and Bosker 1999).

Definition from wikipedia:

Simpsons paradox, or the YuleSimpson effect, is a paradox in probability and statistics, in which a trend that appears in different groups of data but disappears or reverses when these groups are combined. It is sometimes given the impersonal title reversal paradox or amalgamation paradox .

toydat <- data.frame(id = 1:10,
                     group = rep(1:5, each = 2),
                     Y = c(5, 7, 4, 6, 3, 5, 2, 4, 1 ,3),
